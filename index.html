Emissions-Cost Simulation Model

A dynamic, interactive tool that quantifies how emissions affect profitability over time through labor productivity losses and carbon pricing, built using R Shiny.

About This Project

This model aims to demonstrate a hidden cost pathway that many ESG reports overlook — how emissions can gradually undermine a firm’s long-term profitability by reducing labor productivity. Designed with the steel and cement industries in mind, the model lets users test different levers (renewables, offsets, and more) and visualize how emissions trajectories alter output, revenue, and profits over a 10-year horizon.

This model is built in R Shiny and features:

  • Industry selection and scenario toggles
  • Visual plots for emissions, output, revenue, and profits
  • Emissions intensity calculation and optional degradation toggle
  • Side-by-side profit projections with and without emissions impact

How the Model Works

The model starts with assumptions about capital productivity, labor force size, and baseline emissions. When emissions cross certain thresholds, labor productivity drops — which then lowers output growth, reduces revenue, and ultimately alters profitability.

It compares two profit curves:

  • Regular Profit Estimation: Based on output and revenue, without emissions impact.
  • Profit After Emissions Costs: Adjusted to reflect the output drop from productivity loss and rising carbon costs.

This approach brings microeconomic cost theory and ESG impact modeling into a simple, customizable tool.
