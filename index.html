About Me

I’m Sonya, an LSE grad actively tackling real-world sustainability challenges. Right now, I’m deep into shaping strategies for sustainability in high-emitting sectors, building practical models for carbon reduction in industries like steel and cement. I’m also researching how extreme heat impacts productivity in India, using data-driven tools to push the boundaries of climate research. With my background in inclusive development, I’m bringing creative, solution-oriented thinking to every project I work on. Whether that’s shaping ESG strategies, supporting policy change, or engaging communities on climate action. My goal is to bridge the gap between data, policy, and real-world impact, contributing to change that’s both achievable and lasting.

The Capital Correction Project

This initiative models real-world trade-offs between economic growth and decarbonisation. The goal? Equip businesses and policymakers with better insight into emissions, risk pricing, and reduction strategy in hard-to-abate sectors like steel and cement. This is one step toward aligning capital with climate priorities.
